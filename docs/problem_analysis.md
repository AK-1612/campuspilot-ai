# Campus Navigation Failure: Root Cause Analysis

## Problem Statement

Students, faculty members, visitors, and individuals with accessibility needs frequently experience difficulty navigating large campuses due to limitations in existing navigation systems.

## 5 Whys Analysis

### Problem
Users struggle to navigate large campuses efficiently.

### Why 1?
Indoor navigation is often inaccurate.

### Why 2?
Most navigation systems rely heavily on GPS.

### Why 3?
GPS signals become unreliable inside buildings.

### Why 4?
Existing navigation solutions were primarily designed for outdoor environments.

### Why 5?
Campus navigation has traditionally been treated as a mapping problem rather than an accessibility and wayfinding challenge.

## Root Cause

Existing campus navigation systems lack four critical capabilities:

- Accurate indoor positioning
- Accessibility-aware routing
- Real-time environmental awareness
- Reliable offline operation

## Secondary Root Causes

### Accessibility Gap

Most solutions only support basic wheelchair routing and fail to address the needs of visually impaired, hearing-impaired, cognitively diverse, and invisible-disability users.

### Real-Time Information Gap

Route disruptions such as blocked corridors, broken elevators, maintenance activities, and temporary closures are not reflected in navigation systems.

### Connectivity Gap

Many solutions become ineffective during network outages, emergencies, or periods of high congestion.

### Adoption Gap

Users are often reluctant to install dedicated navigation applications for infrequent use.

## Impact

- Lost time
- Increased stress
- Reduced accessibility
- Delayed arrival
- Poor visitor experience
- Lower emergency preparedness

## Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

### Google Maps
Limited indoor navigation support.

### Static Campus Maps
No real-time updates.

### Physical Signage
Requires manual interpretation and local familiarity.

### Accessibility Apps
Typically focus on only one disability category.
